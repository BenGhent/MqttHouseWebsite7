Aledo High School Robotics Club

Aledo Robotics team 6566 was founded in 2012 by a former FLL member. The team experienced a great inaugural season with a PTC Design award for our robot’s scissor lift. The team advanced from our Qualifying Meet to the Regional Championship. Almost all of our team members graduated in 2013, so we experienced a rebuilding year that fall. Our team competed at the Qualifying Meet but did not advance. In 2014, the number of students interested in the team increased so dramatically we formed a second team at our school – FTC 9161. This team’s robot performed well in the qualifying rounds and our alliance of 6566 and 9161 were in 2nd place entering the elimination rounds. Our team continues to grow in knowledge and experience and we expect to have a great season in 2015-2016! This Tradition of fun and excellence was continued on the 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 seasons. During the 2017-18 season team 9161 Overload got the Inspire award at the first qualifier we attended and advanced to the regional competition. There we performed well, but had many technical issues, so we didn’t do as well as the team had hope. We hope to continue this tradition of excellence within our team, and in all three teams that make up the Aledo Robotics Club, in our competition, gracious professionalism, and coopertition this year.

Overload 9161

As a team and as individuals they do many things to bring FIRST and robotics in general unto the community because they think sharing robotics and technology is very important. The things that they do in our community include mentoring two FLL teams, judging at FLL competitions, open sourcing their 3D designs, and doing a robotics summer camp.
